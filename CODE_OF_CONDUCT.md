# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to making participation in this project a welcoming experience for everyone — regardless of disability, age, body size, ethnicity, gender identity, level of experience, nationality, race, religion, or sexual identity.

This project exists to make technology more accessible. We hold ourselves to that same standard in how we work together.

## Our Standards

**Expected behavior:**

- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Center the voices and experiences of people with disabilities
- Respect different viewpoints and experiences
- Accept constructive feedback gracefully
- Focus on what is best for the community

**Unacceptable behavior:**

- Harassment, trolling, or personal attacks
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Making assumptions about what people with disabilities need without involving them
- Using disability simulations or personas without community input and consent
- Other conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Accessibility in Collaboration

- Make meeting materials, documents, and communications accessible
- Provide captions, transcripts, or alternative formats when possible
- Respect different communication styles and needs
- Compensate people with disabilities for their time and expertise

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers at [dcelin@stanford.edu](mailto:dcelin@stanford.edu). All complaints will be reviewed and investigated, and will result in a response appropriate to the circumstances.
